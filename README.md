## Insights
1. Sales Trend Decline: Significant sales drops observed in 2013 and 2015, suggesting external or internal disruptions.
2. Category Performance:
3. Non-Fiction books drive more profit (56%), driven by medical and self-development books
4. Fiction contributes less and has weaker ratings overall
5. Author Performance: Susan Collins emerges as the best-performing author in terms of sales impact.

#### Reader Engagement:
Most Reviewed Book: American Psychological Association (Non-Fiction), reinforcing the trend towards medical/educational topics.

Highest Rated: The 5 Love Languages: The Secret to Love That Lasts (Non-Fiction).

Lowest Rated: Frozen (Little Golden Book) (Fiction).

#### Correlation Analysis:
Price vs Rating : -0.13309  Weak negative correlation (price is not a key factor in book ratings).

#### Customer Preference: 
Higher-rated books generally fall in the higher price range, reflecting a perceived link between price, quality, and satisfaction

### Recommendations
* Address Sales in Declining Years: Investigate 2013 and 2015 drops and reintroduce targeted marketing campaigns.
* Focus on Non-Fiction: Strengthen the Non-Fiction category, particularly in medical, psychology, and self-help niches, as they attract higher ratings and reviews.
* Leverage Best Authors: Promote bestselling authors like Susan Collins through featured collections, campaigns, or loyalty programs.
* Improve Fiction Offerings: Address the low ratings in Fiction by improving content quality, revising book selections, and analyzing customer feedback.
* Pricing Strategy: Since price is not a strong driver of ratings/reviews, consider dynamic pricing models to balance affordability with profitability.
* Boost Reader Engagement: Encourage more reviews for underperforming books to gather deeper insights and build credibility for new releases.
